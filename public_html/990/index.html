General Reading

The following are must have books if you are very interested in model checking. They are great supplements to the material in this course, but I am not requiring them. I have copies of each if you'd like to borrow them, but I will only loan them for short periods of time, e.g., 2 days.
  1. "Model Checking" by Clarke, Grumberg and Peled (available from MIT Press or the usual online book stores)
  2. "The SPIN Model Checker : Primer and Reference Manual", by Holzmann (available online).

Bogor Specific Reading

The research papers on Bogor are collected here. If you haven't alread read the ESEC/FSE 2003 paper "Bogor : An Extensible and Highly-Modular Model Checking Framework" you should do that; other papers will become relevant as we introduce more of the course content.

Overview

The course is broken into five parts:
  1. core state space exploration algorithm (weeks 2-3)
  2. architecture of Bogor (weeks 4-5)
  3. specification checking (weeks 6-7)
  4. reductions and optimizations (weeks 8-11, 12)
  5. project reports (weeks 14-15)

During the course of the semester I'll be linking material into this weekly schedule for your convenience. This material will be drawn from the Software Model Checking : Theory and Practice web-site. There is tons of material there; feel free to explore.
