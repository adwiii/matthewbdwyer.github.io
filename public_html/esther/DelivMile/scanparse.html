Hints

In some cases, it is easier to define a "weaker" grammar rather than trying to enforce the precise constraints in your language
and catching violations of those constraints in latter phases of your compiler. Problematic constraints often arise when
one wants to enforce a ordering on the occurrence of parts of the program input where those parts may be separated by
arbitrary syntax. A classic example of this is the constraint that one can only reference variables that have been previously defined.

In certain situations, you can actually use ANTLR's semantic predicates to encode the checks into the grammar. There is an
example in the SJC parser that you should understand since you may find that technique useful.
